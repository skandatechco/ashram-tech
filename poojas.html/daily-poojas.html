Sacred Ritual Process

Each pooja follows traditional Vedic procedures passed down through generations, ensuring authentic spiritual experience for all devotees.

1

Invocation

The ceremony begins with Ganapati prayers and invocation of Lord Murugan, creating a sacred atmosphere for worship.

2

Abhishekam

Sacred bathing of the deity with water, milk, honey, and other auspicious substances while chanting powerful mantras.

3

Alankaram

Beautiful decoration of the deity with fresh flowers, silk clothes, and ornaments, preparing for the divine darshan.

4

Naivedyam

Offering of food items including fruits, sweets, and cooked meals to the deity with devotion and gratitude.

5

Aarti

The climactic moment with camphor flame offering, accompanied by devotional songs and the ringing of temple bells.

6

Prasadam

Distribution of blessed food and sacred ash to all devotees, completing the worship with divine grace.

Temple Guidelines

Please follow these guidelines to maintain the sanctity of our temple and ensure a peaceful worship experience for all devotees.

Do's

  • Dress modestly and remove footwear before entering
  • Maintain silence during prayers and ceremonies
  • Wash hands and feet before entering the sanctum
  • Follow the priest's instructions during rituals
  • Participate with devotion and respect
  • Turn off mobile phones during worship

Don'ts

  • Don't bring leather items inside the temple
  • Don't take photos without permission
  • Don't touch the deity or sacred items
  • Don't eat or drink inside the prayer hall
  • Don't disturb other devotees during worship
  • Don't leave offerings unattended
